Team Health and Personality Assessment for Jira

Overview

Team Health and Personality Assessment for Jira helps teams review workplace collaboration, work-style preferences, and team-level health indicators inside Jira. The app provides Big Five, DISC, and MBTI-compatible questionnaires, individual result pages, aggregate team analytics, and executive-level filters for organization-wide review.

The app is designed for workplace insight, coaching, communication planning, and team support. It is not intended for clinical diagnosis, medical assessment, hiring decisions, performance scoring, or automated employment decisions.

Installation

  1. Install the app from Atlassian Marketplace or through an approved Forge installation link.
  2. Confirm the requested Jira scopes during installation.
  3. Open Jira and go to Apps.
  4. Select Team Health Assessment.
  5. Jira administrators can open the app from Jira administration settings to configure privacy and reassessment options.

My Results

The My Results page gives each user a personal summary of their latest assessment results, including Big Five workplace signal, DISC workstyle signal, MBTI-compatible preference pattern, reassessment schedule, and work pressure trend summary.

Answer Questions

The Answer Questions page allows users to complete Big Five, DISC, and MBTI-compatible questionnaires. Administrators can configure how often users are allowed or required to reassess.

Team Analytics

The Team Analytics page shows aggregate results for authorized users, including response coverage, privacy threshold status, MBTI distribution, user personality index, and aggregate team-level signals.

Executive Filters

The Executive page is designed for organization-level review and planning. Available filters include scope, project, team, role, test type, and pressure level. Executive charts update based on the selected test type and filters.

Privacy And Responsible Use

The app uses privacy thresholds to prevent small-group analytics from exposing identifiable results. Results are workplace and collaboration indicators only and must not be used for clinical diagnosis, hiring decisions, or direct employment decisions.
